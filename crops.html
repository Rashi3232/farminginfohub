Popular Crops

    Wheat

    A staple crop grown worldwide, ideal for cooler climates.

    Rice

    Mainly grown in wetlands and requires a lot of water.

    Maize (Corn)

    Widely grown in temperate regions and used for food and feed.

    Soybean

    A major source of protein and oil, ideal for warm climates.

    Barley

    Commonly grown for animal feed and brewing beer.

    Potato

    A root vegetable that grows best in cool weather.

    Sugarcane

    Needs a tropical climate and is used to produce sugar and ethanol.

    Tomato

    A versatile crop that can be grown in fields or greenhouses.

    Peanut

    Legume that grows underground and is rich in oil and protein.

    Cotton

    Widely grown for fiber production, needs warm and dry climate.

    Millet

    A drought-resistant grain often grown in arid regions.

    Chickpea

    Commonly used in pulses and high in protein.

    Sorghum

    A hardy crop grown in dry regions, used for food and fodder.

    Onion

    A widely used vegetable crop that grows well in temperate zones.

    Mustard

    Grown for its oil-rich seeds and leafy greens.

    Sunflower

    Grown for edible oil and seeds, requires good sunlight.

    Carrot

    A root crop, rich in beta-carotene and vitamins.

    Beans

    A legume crop that improves soil fertility and provides protein.

    Pumpkin

    A large fruit vegetable, great for pies and soups.

    Garlic

    Grown for its strong flavor and health benefits.
